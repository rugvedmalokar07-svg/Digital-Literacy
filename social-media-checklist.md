# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

Navigating social media responsibly is crucial for maintaining a positive digital footprint. Here is a checklist of 5 Do's and 5 Don'ts to keep in mind:

## ✅ The Do's

* **Do think before you post:** Always assume that future employers, internship coordinators, and professors might see your public posts. 
* **Do utilize privacy settings:** Regularly review and adjust your privacy settings to control who has access to your personal life and photos.
* **Do engage respectfully:** Treat others with respect in comment sections and direct messages, even if you disagree with their opinions.
* **Do verify information:** Check the credibility of a source before sharing articles or news to avoid spreading misinformation.
* **Do curate a professional presence:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to highlight your academic achievements, projects, and career aspirations positively.

## ❌ The Don'ts

* **Don't overshare personal details:** Avoid posting sensitive information like your home address, phone number, class schedule, or financial details.
* **Don't post offensive or illegal content:** Never share content that is discriminatory, harassing, or depicts illegal activities. 
* **Don't engage in cyberbullying:** Never use social media to intimidate, embarrass, or harass your peers.
* **Don't vent about academic frustrations publicly:** Avoid complaining about your university, professors, or classmates online, as this leaves a highly unprofessional trail.
* **Don't rely on the "delete" button:** Remember that screenshots exist; once something is on the internet, you lose control over where it goes, even if you delete the original post.
