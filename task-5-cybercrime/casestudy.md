# Case Study: The "QR Code Receive Money" UPI Scam

**What the crime is:**
This is a form of UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud where scammers trick victims into authorizing a payment under the false pretence of *receiving* money for an online sale.

**How it typically happens:**
1. **The Setup:** A college student lists a second-hand item (like a textbook or a bicycle) for sale on an online marketplace or a campus WhatsApp group.
2. **The Bait:** The scammer contacts the student, posing as an eager buyer. They agree to the asking price immediately without any negotiation to create a sense of excitement and urgency.
3. **The Trap:** The scammer claims they cannot pay in cash and insists on using UPI. Instead of sending money to the student's number, the scammer sends a QR code or a "Payment Request" link via WhatsApp. 
4. **The Execution:** The scammer tells the student, "Scan this QR code and enter your UPI PIN to receive the funds into your account." 
5. **The Fraud:** Eager to close the sale and misunderstanding how UPI works, the student enters their PIN. Because a PIN is only ever used to *send* money, the funds are instantly deducted from the student's account and transferred to the scammer.

**Who is usually targeted:**
College students and young adults who are trying to sell items online quickly. Scammers prey on their urgency and slight misunderstandings of digital payment mechanics.

**The Consequences:**
The victim suffers an immediate financial loss, which can be devastating on a student budget. It also causes severe emotional distress, embarrassment, and anxiety about future digital transactions.
