# Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for College Students

Navigating the digital world safely requires constant vigilance. Here are 8 actionable steps you can take to protect your data and your wallet:

## Financial & UPI Safety 💸
1. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money:** Your UPI PIN is only required when you are *sending* money or checking your balance. You never need a PIN to receive funds.
2. **Verify the receiver before paying:** Always double-check the VPA (Virtual Payment Address) and the registered name on your UPI app screen before hitting send. 

## General Digital Security 🔒
3. **Use strong, unique passwords:** Avoid using the same password for your student portal, email, and social media. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
4. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for all your critical accounts. Even if a hacker gets your password, they won't be able to log in without the second verification step.
5. **Beware of urgent phishing links:** Do not click on unverified links in SMS or WhatsApp messages claiming your "bank account is blocked," your "package is delayed," or you "won a prize."
6. **Keep your software updated:** Regularly update your phone's operating system and apps. These updates often contain critical security patches that protect against new vulnerabilities.
7. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks:** Never perform banking transactions, UPI transfers, or log into sensitive accounts while connected to open, unsecured campus or cafe Wi-Fi networks.
8. **Verify "emergency" requests from friends:** If a friend or family member urgently asks for money via social media or WhatsApp, call them on the phone to verify. Their account may have been hacked.

## 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you or someone you know becomes a victim of cybercrime, report it immediately:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** Visit [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Cybercrime Helpline:** Dial **1930** (Available 24x7)
